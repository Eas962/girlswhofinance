Covered Calls and Options

What is an Option?

An option is a derivative (think Latin roots, not math), which means that it is not the actual stock, but a form of it that moves based on the actual stock (called an underlying).

What is a Covered Call?

A covered call is a trading strategy where you own a stock and sell options on it. This is an easy low risk way to start trading options while also improving your profit.

For example, you own 100 shares of Apple which is trading at $155/share. You are willing to sell the stock if it reaches $160 so you sell a call option for $160. You might receive $2.35 per share for this option, or a total of $235. What happens next?

The $235 is yours to keep no matter what the price of Apple stock is when the option expires.

If Apple stock is below $160 nothing else happens and you are free to sell a new call option in the next period and make another $235.

If Apple is above $160 the option will be “exercised” and you will sell your stock for $160 per share. In this case you made $500 gain when the stock moved from $155 to $160, plus $235 for the option. A total profit of $735.

You Can Also Use Options in Place of Stock

100 shares of Apple stock trading at $155/share would cost $15,500, but an at the money option for 100 shares would only cost $500. If Apple goes up $5 a share, you make the same profit of $500 on the option as you would on 100 shares of stock. That is 3% profit if you own the stock, but 100% if you own the option.

What is the Risk of an Option?

If the stock does not go up before the option expires your option expires worthless and you are out the $500.

Options are a powerful tool to improve your investing results. Take the time to educate yourself!
